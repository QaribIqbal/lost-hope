PRIVATE LOG • HEART
By Qarib Iqbal
How I outlived my own heart.
A single page about locking, burning, losing, and still moving.
I am not a poet, and I never planned to become one... this only exists because someone once talked to me and said, “you’re good with words.” That sentence is the reason this entire poem was written.
"I locked my heart inside a steel tomb...
not to bury it,
but to stop the bleeding.
So the hollow shell of me
could somehow stay upright.
I clothed myself in armor...
coldness stitched with numbness...
so no one could see
how little of me was left inside.
Not because I feared people.
Not because I feared pain.
But because survival,
sometimes, demands silence.
I once let love bloom freely...
first in innocence,
then in one‑sided devotion,
then in a fire I mistook for destiny.
I learned to turn love into anger,
just to protect my pride.
I convinced myself it worked.
And for a long time,
no one felt worthy
of what I no longer allowed myself to feel.
Then love found me again...
real, mutual, alive.
And just when my guarded heart
began to thaw,
duty tightened its grip,
and I had to let her go.
Something didn’t just break...
it hardened.
Now I do not fear love.
I simply do not trust
that I am allowed to keep it.
And so I remain...
armored, breathing,
moving forward.
Relentless.
But not untouched."
PRIVATE LOG • PUPPET
By Qarib Iqbal
Wooden Puppet // Borrowed Life
A page about becoming everything for everyone, until nothing remains for yourself.
This tablet is carved for the version of me that tried to be a home for everyone, and forgot to leave a room for himself.
I tried to be everything
for everyone I called my own...
the ones I thought would stay,
would hold me through each rise and fall,
my family, my brother.
But somewhere in the trying,
I misplaced my soul,
lost the scent of who I was.
By the time I turned to look,
I was gone... a faint memory
dressed in skin and obedience.
Now I sway like a wooden puppet,
my strings tangled in their hands,
dancing to voices that aren’t mine.
My heart... if any still beats within...
no longer dares to dream.
It never mattered what I lost;
I gave away my peace,
my safe place, my pieces of joy,
and told myself it was fine.
Yet the agony lies here...
even after the giving, the emptying,
there is no gratitude,
only hunger for another sacrifice.
So I’ve learned to survive, my friend,
not as a man, not as a soul,
but as a puppet...
wood painted with a smile,
heart carved out in trade for their approval...
surviving, yes,
but no longer alive.
Pretending it doesn’t hurt anymore,
moving to keep the peace,
that never truly was.
